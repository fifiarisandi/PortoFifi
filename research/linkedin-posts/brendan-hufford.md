## Post 2 — June 8, 2026
**Link:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/brendanhufford_every-piece-of-lazy-ai-content-says-the-same-share-7464727802991284224-dEnW/
**Format:** Text

Every piece of lazy AI content says the same thing to your reader: your time is worth less than mine.

That's the implicit message. Every single freaking time.

You should take the time to consume this.

But it's not worth *my* time to create it for you.

And look, I think this is a quality debate, not a tactics debate.

But asking "is this good enough?" is the wrong question.

Good enough for what? To fool somebody? Is that what the goal of your marketing is? To fool people?

It's a respect debate now, too.

Low-quality content used to be rare because it was (more) expensive to produce.

You had to pay a writer.

You had to care at least a little bit.

Now it feels free. 20 bucks a month. Unlimited LinkedIn posts. Endless blogs.

So the floor completely fell out.

Things like 5 em dashes in a post are symptoms.

The disease is entitlement to someone else's attention.

Because I'm starting to feel like the default output of every AI tool is: sound smart, say nothing, respect nobody's time.

If you're using AI to write, great.

If you're using AI to avoid writing, your audience already knows.

⚡

PS - I even used AI to "write" this post.

Every week, a Claude skill integrated with Fathom's MCP pulls all my calls from the last 7 days.

It analyzes them using Erica Schneider's MP4 process and reminds me of all the smart stuff I said (that I already forgot because I've had 87 baseball and soccer games in that timeframe).

I'm using AI to create better. Not to care less.
